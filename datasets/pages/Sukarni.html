Sukarni (14 July 1916-7 May 1971) was one of young Indonesian activists who demanded independence during Dutch colonial governments and Japanese imperial army occupation and the chairman of Murba until his death.

Biography[edit]

Sukarni in 1960

Sukarni was born on 14 July 1916 in Sumberdiran, Garum, Blitar. The name Sukarni in Javanese means "to pay more attention".[1] He was the fifth child of Dimun Kartodiwirjo, son of Diponegoro's right hand man Onggomerto, and Supiah, a woman from Kediri.[2][3] Sukarni's older siblings were Hono, Sukarlim, Sukarmilah, Sukardi, while the younger siblings were Suparti, Endang Sarti, Endi Sukarto, Sukarjo, and unnamed person who died as a child.[4][5] Sukarni studied at Mardisiswo in Blitar, similar school to Taman Siswa that time.[6] Sukarni continued to study at HIS.[7] When Sukarni graduated from HIS and registered for MULO, his father died in 1929.[6] After graduating from MULO, Sukarni then continued his study to Kweekschool (teacher school) and Volks Universiteit (People University).[7]

According to Emalia Iragiliati, Sukarni once had become Sukarno's disciple in Bandung studying journalism, encouraged by Sukarno elder sister, Wardoyo, in Blitar.[8][9]

In 1930, Sukarni joined Perhimpunan Pemuda Pelajar Indonesia, which was later renamed to Indonesia Muda, and became the chairman of Blitar section.[10][11] He also joined Persatuan Pemuda Rakyat Indonesia (Perpri) section Purworejo. In 1933 he became the chairman of Indonesia Muda section Purworejo.[12] Due to his activities in Indonesia Muda, Sukarni was dropped out of MULO but he was allowed to take special examination so he still graduated.[13] Sukarni once created PPPI's rival, Persatuan Pemuda Kita because the former didn't allow youths who didn't go to school to join. After creating this, later both groups were merged and renamed to Indonesia Muda.[11] In Indonesia Muda congress in Surabaya, Sukarni managed to persuade congress participants to approve his idea allowing youths that didn't go to school to join Indonesia Muda.[14] In 1934, he was appointed as the chairman of Pengurus Besar Indonesia Muda.[15] Sukarni helped Anwari and elder sister of Sukarno, Wardoyo, to establish Partindo section Blitar and for the first time met Wikana, Asmara Hadi, and SK Trimurti.[16] to In the end of 1935, Sukarni was appointed as chairman of Perpri section Jakarta.[12] In 1936, Sukarni translated Tan Malaka's brochures and pamphlets from Dutch to Indonesian and distributed them.[17] He managed to escape arrest of Indonesia Muda members in the head office in Jakarta on 19 June 1936.[18] Colonial government searched Sukarni due to his article in Indonesia Muda magazine encouraging youths to united against colonialism.[19]

Sukarni who was pursued by government then decided to go to Borneo. He said goodbye to his mother in Blitar and entrusted Indonesia Muda leadership to Ruslan Abdulgani.[20] Sukarni was in disguise under the name Maidi in Borneo area from 1937 to 1940. He traveled to Banjarmasin then moved to Murung Pudak working as coolie at Bataafsche Petroleum Maatschappij. He then moved to Sangasanga worked as servant at one of BPM official house. He then moved to Balikpapan and served as opnemer in BPM topography department. He was then promoted to leerling opnemer.[21]

Sukarni eventually was arrested by colonial government there before Japanese attack Dutch East Indies, some times in 1940 according to Husein Yusuf,[22] while in disguise as the employee of the department of BPM.[23] He was brought to Samarinda before jailed in Java, which was chosen over Boven Digul because Japanese army had attacked Dutch Indies.[24] Sukarni was then brought to Jakarta via Surabaya.[22] Sukarni initially was jailed in Pasar Baru prison as European which may freely moved in daytime.[25] Sukarni was then released and lived with Antara staffs.[26]

Sukarni, people from Antara such as Adam Malik, A.M. Sipahutar, Pandu Kartawiguna, and Abdul Hakim, and other activists such as Asmara Hadi, Mulia, Wasdji Kartawiganda, and Wikana were arrested in December 1941 because Dutch colonial government considered them as dangerous people.[26] At first they were jailed in Pasar Baru prison. They then moved to Sukabumi, then Garut then Sukabumi again.[27] Before transferred to Australia, they were then moved to Nusakambangan on 2 March 1942 and settled there.[28][27] The transport ship which they were deported with named KPM Tawali along with other ships docked there then were destroyed by Japanese attacks on 4 and 5 March.[29] On 10th, all of the prisoners were set free including Sukarni, Adam Malik, and Wikana.[24][27] On 15th, Sukarni and the others traveled to Maos by foot. They then crossed Serayu River then walked again to Jeruklegi. There they went to Jakarta using train from Bandung. In this train, Sukarni met Nursjiar, his future wife.[30]

In Jakarta, Sukarni worked for several news agency such as Antara, which was later replaced by Domei, and Sendenbu.[31][24] During this time, according to Adam Malik, and according to Emalia Iragiliati that time was 1942, Sukarni married Nursjiar.[32][33] Sukarni and the other young men worked in Sendenbu and other Japanese office to infiltrate and find information.[34]

Sukarni persuaded chairman of Perhimpunan Pelajar-Pelajar Indonesia Chairul Saleh and chairman of Baperpi Supeno to establish educational institution for Indonesian young men. In the end of 1942 the Ashrama[a] Angkatan Baru or Ashrama Pemuda at Menteng 31 was founded and aided by Sendenbu.[36] In 1943, the first two were responsible for organizing the institution as chairman and vice chairman respectively helped by A.M Hanafi as secretary and I. Wiejaja as treasurer.[24][37]

In this period, Sukarni also had some connection to Tan Malaka who worked for Japanese factory in Bayah, Banten, at the time.[31][37] Sukarni had been visited by Husin, aliases of Tan Malaka, which Sukarni not knew about. They discussed about politics for one day before Husin visited other activists such as Chairul Saleh and Tjokroaminoto which Husin hadn't met, and Achmad Subardjo.[38]

Sukarni became father in 1943 after Nursjiar gave birth Luhantara, their first son. A year later, they had another son, Kumalakanta.[39]

Sukarni took part in Kongres Pemuda meeting held between 16 and 18 May 1945 in Villa Isola, Bandung. The desire of the young men increased after the meeting especially in Java.[40] In the year Sukarni was also the chairman of Markas Besar Committee van Actie Menteng 31.[41]

On 3 June 1945, Sukarni and 32 other young men held a meeting at Gedung Gambir Selatan. The result of the meeting was the appointment of 10 committee members led by B.M. Diah and Sukarni, Sudiro, Sjarif Thajeb, Harsono Tjokroaminoto, Wikana, Chairul Saleh, Gultom, Supeno, and Asmara Hadi, who were responsible for the talk of the formation of Gerakan Angkatan Baru Indonesia which was founded on 15 June.[42]

Sometimes in PPKI meeting, the youth had some problem with the old. Sukarni and Chairul Saleh were transferred due to this event.[43]

Ashrama 31 was then dissolved on 15 August 1945 immediately after Japanese army surrendered.[44] The activists from various youth groups then formed Committee van Actie headed by Sukarni, Chairul Saleh and Wikana as vices chairman, Adam Malik, Pandu Kartawiguna, Maruto Nitimihardjo, Djohar Nur, Darwis, A.M. Hanafi, and Armunanto as members.[45]

At Sukarno's house on the night of 15 August, young men urged him to declare the independence of Indonesia. According to Sukarno in his autobiography, Sukarni was there urging him, but according to Sukarni, that time he was at Cikini 71 waiting the result of the discussion.[46] The young men didn't want the independence as a present from Japanese.[47] During the wait, Sukarni, dr Muwardi, Dr. Sutjipto, Chairul Saleh, Wikana, Jusuf Kunto, and the other planned the abduction of Sukarno-Hatta.[48] Sukarni talked to Sukarno supporting the other young men arguments to declare the independence when they began the abduction the dawn 16th.[46]

Sukarni, Singgih, Muwardi, and Jusuf Kunto conducted the abduction of Sukarno-Hatta to Rengasdengklok while Wikana and Chairul Saleh waited in Jakarta.[49] Latief Hendradiningrat from Peta borrowed the youths military uniforms and pistols to avoid Japanese army inspection.[50] During the trip, Sukarni and Kunto accompanied Hatta while Sukarno, his wife Fatmawati, and their son Guntur in the other car. They arrived in Jakarta evening same day after Achmad Subardjo followed them and managed to persuade young men to go home.[51][49]

Sukarno and Hatta were brought to Rear Admiral Maeda's house. Sukarni stated that he already prepared the revolt in several places in Jakarta. Maeda disagreed the revolt because it was more danger than plain proclamation. Sukarni, accompanied by Sayuti Melik and Maeda's subordinate Nishijima, then informed people that were ready to cancel the revolt.[52]

Sukarni and the other young men were at the house during the writing of proclamation manuscript.[49] Sukarni advised the other to choose only Sukarno and Hatta who signed the proclamation manuscript instead of all people who were there that time.[53]

After KNIP was given the authority as the legislative on 17 October, Sukarni demanded the reformation of the organization and appointed Sjahrir as the chairman instead of Kasman Singodimejo and as the formateur of Badan Pekerja KNIP.[54] In November, Sukarni joined Chairul Saleh-led Badan Kongres Pemuda in Yogyakarta.[55]

In 1946, Sukarni's third child, Parialuti Indarwati, was born.[56] On 31 March 1946, Persatuan Perdjuangan leaders were arrested by government authorities ordered by Sjahrir including Tan Malaka, secretary general Sukarni, and Sayuti Melik.[57] In 1947, Sukarni was imprisoned in Ponorogo prison, Madiun, along with Tan Malaka, Moh. Yamin, and Ahmad Subardjo. Sukarni and Tan Malaka were then transferred to Magelang prison.[58] They were released in September 1948 from the prison.[59]

In November 1948, Sukarni was elected as chairman of Murba Party in the first congress mainly suggested by Tan Malaka.[60] Mainly Sukarni-led Murba opposed the government policies especially Linggarjati, Renville, and KMB, except the decree reimposing 1945 Constitution.[61]

Sukarni managed to escape from Yogyakarta down the Code River after the second military aggression was started.[62] Sukarni and the other traveled to Imogiri then Gunungkidul escaping from Dutch army.[63] Outside Yogyakarta, Sukarni helped Lieutenant Colonel Suharto carrying out guerrilla attacks.[64] During these time, Sukarni strangely disguised under the name Suharto.[65]

In 1949, Nursjiar gave birth to the fourth child, Goos Murbantoro.[56] In 1952, Sukarni and Husein Yusuf traveled to Balikpapan to met the former old friends during at work at BPM.[22] In 1953, Sukarni's last child, Emalia Iragiliati was born in Yogyakarta.[66]

After general election in 1955, Sukarni was appointed as member of legislature, Konstituante, and Badan Pekerja KNIP.[66]

Sukarni was appointed by Sukarno as ambassador for People's Republic of China mid 1960.[67] The leadership of Murba was passed to Wasid Suwarto. Sukarni then returned in March 1964.[68][69]

After Indonesian's foreign diplomacy was inclined to Peking and communism, Sukarni was arrested on 5 January 1965 charged with coup d'Ã©tat and Sukarno's assassination attempt using Badan Pendukung Sukarnoisme,[70] while according to Hasyim Darif, Sukarni was arrested because of his action against PKI.[71][69] According to A. Hamzah Tuppu, Sukarno was provoked by Aidit.[72] He was detained in detention center in Kejaksaan Agung RI office.[70] After the power transition to New Order, in 1966 Sukarni was released.[73]

Sukarni became the chairman of Murba after the party had been prohibited during Sukarni's detainment.[74] When a rumor spread that Sukarno was about to be brought to military court, Sukarni asked Hamzah Tuppu personally to prevent it. Sukarni reasoned that if Sukarno was imprisoned, the republic would end because it had no dignity mentioning detainee every year in independence celebration.[75]

Sukarni was elected as Fourth Chairman of Dewan Harian Nasional (what's it in English?) of Angkatan '45 after its first congress in April(pp. 258-9, according to Mayjen. M. Jasin) or September 1966 in Gelora Senayan.[76] Sukarni then involved in Delegasi '45 along with other Angkatan '45 to persuade Sukarno to disband PKI to avoid civil war.[77]

In 1967, Sukarni became the member of Dewan Pertimbangan Agung.[78]

In 1970, Sukarni and Rasjidi involved in Musyawarah Besar Angkatan '45 in Berastagi, North Sumatera.[79]

Some times in 1971, Sukarni was admitted to Cipto Mangunkusumo Hospital.[80]

Before 1971 general election, Sukarni persuade other social and political elements to join Murba.[81] On 5 May 1971, Sukarni requested Darif to write text to speech to his campaign in general election which they met in the evening that day. Two days later on 7th, Sukarni died in early day in Jakarta.[82] His remains were buried in Taman Makam Pahlawan Kalibata.[83]

Political view[edit]

Sukarni considered BPUPKI was dominated by Indonesians while PPKI was dominated by Japanese military-backed old activists.[84]

According to Hasyim Darif, Sukarni was influenced by Sukarno mainly during the underground activities against late Dutch colonial government and Japanese military government when the two met and discussed politics, but Sukarni was against Sukarno during the National Revolution.[85] Sukarni was also influenced by Tan Malaka during Dutch and Japanese era through Tan Malaka's writings. After the proclamation, they met with clear identities, not aliases. Tan Malaka influenced Sukarni both political theory and practices during National Revolution. Both of them opposed Indonesian government compromising with the Dutch including Linggarjati, Renville, and KMB.[86] Khalid Rasjidi described Sukarni as "the most loyal and most trusted Tan Malaka's disciple."[87] After Sukarno declared the Nasakom way, Sukarni was against PKI political activities.[71]

Moh. Padang stated that "Murba was Sukarni, Sukarni was Murba; without Sukarni Murba didn't exist."[88]

Iragiliati citing Sukarni that Indonesian murba (musyawarah rakyat banyak; deliberation of common people) is different than western version. Indonesian version prioritizes family relationship.[17]

Roeslan Abdulgani described the political view of Sukarni as "radical-revolutionary" while his way of life "filled with the soul of democracy".[89]

Legacy[edit]

Sukarni was awarded posthumously Bintang Maha Putera IV.[90] According to the Emalia Iragiliati, youngest daughter of Sukarni, Sukarni was able to speak Javanese, Indonesian, Dutch, German, and Japanese.[91]

An exhibition about Sukarni and GSSJ Ratulangie was held from 6 until 30 August 2009 in Museum Perumusan Naskah Proklamasi, Jakarta.[92]

On 7 November 2014, Government of Indonesia awarded Sukarni National Hero title along with Djamin Gintings, Mohammad Mangoendiprojo, and Abdul Wahab Chasbullah by Presidential Decree No. 115/TK/2014.[93]
